Behavioural Skills

A behavioural skill is a way of working that you will use in many on-the-job activities. You will need to learn these skills to be successful on the job.

Behavior is not limited to your actions; it also determines your chain of thoughts and your attitudes. Human behavior is experienced throughout an individual’s lifetime. It includes the way they act based on various factors such as genetics, faith, attitude, and social norms. Communication skills like assertiveness, clarity, and consistency make a difference in how someone is perceived by others.

  • Adaptability: Adaptability means to adjust one’s actions to each situation, even when in a changing environment.
  • Proactiveness: Proactiveness means taking the lead on your work without any push from your manager, and anticipating situations and preempting actions that would be needed.
  • Communication: Communication is a two-way street that enables us to send and receive information.
  • Teamwork: Teamwork is when a group of people, or just two people work together towards a shared goal.
